1. Introduction of amagi Live

Amagi LIVE is a UHD ready, cloud-based live orchestration platform. It helps content creators and broadcasters spin-up live channels instantly and orchestrate broadcast-grade live content on the go. Amagi LIVE can be deployed with ease and is capable of ingesting multi-source, multi-protocol content including NDI IP standard. The platform is all for flexibility and enables the operator to control break durations and slots, insert graphics in the live feed, extend live playout, switch between input sources and so on with live controls on the platform. Since Amagi LIVE is completely cloud-based, it can be operated remotely from anywhere in the world, using a simple web dashboard with multiple operation modes.

A live event broadcast may playout as per plan, or may have disruptions because of unplanned delays or unforeseen issues. An operator or broadcaster may or may not be able to play the scheduled playlist as planned. The features described here will support the operator to take appropriate action during the live playout.

As an operator for a live event, you may encounter any of the following situations:

  • Live source(s) not ready to stream due to technical issues
  • Delay in the start of the event
  • Event going beyond the planned duration
  • Inability to play a planned break due to certain circumstances in the live event
  • Input from the director / MCR to switch sources, introduce unscheduled breaks, repeat an already played promo or break etc

All these mandate that you be able to view the playlist, edit if needed and publish the updated playlist during the live playout. You can use the CLOUDPORT LIVE or DYNAMIC UI for this purpose.

1.1 amagi CLOUDPORT LIVE

CLOUDPORT LIVE is a 24x7 linear playout that has both file-based and live shows. It offers the ability to ingest content from multiple live sources and play out live streams while the Digital Video Effects (DVE) and advanced graphics make the experience even richer for the user.

Amagi CLOUDPORT is the most versatile playout platform available today and can ingest all industry-preferred formats for audio, video, and subtitles. It can generate output in multiple formats such as HLS, RTMP, and HD-SDI, and can also be used for VOD delivery to vMVPD platforms.

Operators can use amagi CLOUDPORT LIVE to schedule a 24-hour playlist with live segments interspersed with file-based playouts.  These live segments could be sports events, studio shows, news hours, live debates, award ceremonies, key addresses from heads of state, etc.

What can you do with amagi CLOUDPORT LIVE?

CLOUDPORT LIVE provides all aspects of live channel requirements like live controls for ad insertion, graphics, logos, cloud-based media asset management, scheduling and multi-feed playout monitoring. This is useful for broadcasters who want to mix live and file-based playout content in their channel.

1.2 amagi DYNAMIC

Traditionally live events (e.g. sports, music concerts etc) are programmed and streamed as part of a TV Channel. The audience switches to a specific TV channel for viewing the live event as per the schedule. With OTT streaming gaining currency among the audience, there is a requirement to stream live events that are not associated with a TV channel.

Amagi DYNAMIC is an on-demand, live playout system that can be used to orchestrate a live event end-to-end. . Additionally, DYNAMIC allows multiple live events to be streamed in parallel. DYNAMIC enables operators to stream live content such as sporting events and matches, reality shows, and talent contests as standalone content for their OTT apps. .

Take the FIFA World Cup as an example, where several matches are sometimes scheduled at the same time . These matches can be fully managed and orchestrated (with event and sponsor branding, logo, graphics, commercial breaks, and  triggers) using DYNAMIC and distributed through OTT for viewers to consume.

Here are a few typical use cases for Amagi DYNAMIC:

  • sporting events [e.g. European Premier League, University events, Formula One etc]
  • news [ e.g. presidential address, public rallies etc]
  • enterprises [e.g. product launches, CEO address etc]
  • entertainment [e.g. music concerts, dance shows etc]

 

What can you do with amagi DYNAMIC?

  • Stream concurrent events simultaneously to OTT apps without needing separate channels for each live stream: Unlike amagi CLOUDPORT LIVE, DYNAMIC allows you to live stream content independent  of linear channels.
  • Stream Live content from one or more sources : You can have upto 8 sources and choose between them.
  • On-demand: DYNAMIC spins-up the live streaming infrastructure on-the-fly as per the live event schedule, thereby driving down the overall cost.
  • End-to-end orchestrated: You can schedule your Live events, start streaming at the scheduled time, include breaks, graphics and ads and automatically wind down once the Live events conclude. All these are orchestrated dynamically in the cloud.

1.4 Understanding Live Event, Live Feed and Live Segment

Live Event is an event which is a currently happening occurrence that is being streamed. For example:- sports matches, awards or a ceremony.

Live Feed

Refers to the play out of a live event in different channels to cater to targeted or language-specific ads or studio/voice over content. An event can be played out on multiple feeds.

Live Segment

Refers to the live feed as an item of the playlist, which is segmented (made into chunks) to play interspersed with breaks. 

1.5 CLOUDPORT LIVE and DYNAMIC - LIVE CONTROLS

1.6 Live segment mode

➽Switch to the live segment [CLOUDPORT LIVE]

You can switch to the live segment using the TAKE LIVE button. At this time, you should also choose the input source for the live segment.

➽Live segment will always be ON [DYNAMIC]

Live segments will continue to play even beyond the segment duration in the playlist. As an operator, you have to choose actions from the button bank [TAKE NEXT, UNHOLD] to play the assets in the scheduled playlist.

1.7 Playout of the non-live segments in the playlist

When a non-live segment [SCTE break/promo/graphics] is played during a live show, it plays out its scheduled duration and moves to the next item on the playlist by default.

As an operator, you can choose to either play the asset in a loop, skip playing the scheduled asset by playing a specific asset scheduled at a later time or unloop a looped asset. You can also introduce an unscheduled break [a SCTE break or graphic] based on the situation.

1.8 Choosing between various live sources

Upto 8 live sources can be configured for a live show. As an operator, you can switch seamlessly between any of the 8 sources for the playout.

1.9 Preview the incoming input source

During a live event, the input sources or streams may originate from multiple places. They could be from a studio where hosts are seated or from the area where action is [e.g. matches, games, contests etc]. In the case of video assets, these are played from the media library.

As an operator, you can monitor the health of these input sources/streams so that a particular input source may be played out depending on the situation using the preview widget.

1.10 When the live show is ON

As an operator, you can choose any one of the following actions based on input from the event director or depending on the live event’s progress or based on the health of the asset as shown in the preview widget:

  • Stop the live segment to play a scheduled SCTE/breaks/graphics/promos
  • Stop the live segment to play an unscheduled SCTE/breaks/graphics/promos using the Shotbox
  • Play a specific asset in the scheduled playlist
  • Play the asset continuously in a loop
  • Stop the asset in the loop and move to the next item in the playlist
  • Play the next asset in the playlist
  • Replay an asset that has already been played
  • Play an asset that was skipped previously
  • Return to playing the live segment
  • Disable an asset in the playlist

1.11 Unscheduled Breaks

Typically, playlists have a scheduled list of assets to be played for a planned duration.

However, as an operator, you can play a set of graphics which are not part of the playlist using the shotbox feature after interrupting the live segment. These are played :

  • for dynamic ad insertion [DAI] for region specific promos and commercials.
  • when an interruption in input live streaming occurs
  • when textual information regarding the live event (e.g: player statistics during a match) has to be shown.
  • when countdowns for an event have to be displayed
  • when customers are targeted based on region, preferences etc.
  • when real time texts from social media have to be played.
  • as a promo or a coming up banner for another event
  • for a countdown for an event

1.12 Joining the playlist after a live segment

Typically, live playlists have a scheduled list of live segments and assets to be played out for a planned duration. However, due to inherent issues in a live event, the actual live segment may either finish earlier than scheduled or can end later than scheduled or as directed by the event director.

As an operator, you can decide how the subsequent items on the scheduled playlist are played out after a live segment by

  • using a marker (marking an asset) to indicate the next item to be played
  • using specific actions using the button bank by playing a specific item on the playlist [MANUAL TAKE] or the next item [TAKE NEXT]

When the live segment finishes earlier than planned [CLOUDPORT LIVE]

  • Play the scheduled assets post the live event until the start of the next program. You can update the playlist manually to identify assets to play for the remainder of the event duration.
  • Play Rescue until the start of the next program.

When the live show finishes earlier than planned [DYNAMIC]

  • Play Rescue until the end of the live event.

When a live segment ends later than planned [CLOUDPORT LIVE]

  • Play the scheduled assets post the live event until the start of the next program, if there is time until the next program.
  • Or you can select the asset to be played by manually identifying the asset using the MANUAL TAKE action.
  • If there is no time for the start of the next program, then start the next program and this will have an abrupt ending.

When a live segment ends later than planned [DYNAMIC]

  • Exit the live show.

1.13 Exit Live Streaming

As an operator, you may want to exit in the middle of the Live event because

  • the actual event has ended ahead of schedule
  • you receive instructions from the event director.

You have the option to exit the live segment after playing a break (either a scheduled break or an ad hoc break). Exiting after playing a break will provide a more smooth exit to the viewers than closing in an abrupt manner.

1.14 Operator friendly user interface

(i) Single UI to view, edit and publish the playlist

You have a single UI for both playlist view and edit. Once you edit the playlist, you can publish it directly [CLOUDPORT LIVE] from the edit mode. In DYNAMIC, it is auto-published.

For CLOUDPORT LIVE, you can use the same UI for file-based playout as well.

(ii) Timers

During a live playout, you have countdown timers on the screen to get your attention about certain milepost events that are happening. Based on these timers, you can choose your next course of action.

These timers are:

  • Time left till end of current segment
  • Time left till end of current show
  • Time left till start of next live segment
  • Time till start of next SCTE
  • Time till start of next marked

(iii) Widgets to assist the operator

You can use the Playing Now widget to confirm what is being played out and to choose the right time to introduce an unordered break. You can also detect issues in the channel output so that you can intervene, to view various parameters such as audio decibel level, SCTE information, video attributes, etc.

You can also see the timer that will indicate the time for the next item in the playlist [either a scheduled break or the live segment].

You can use the Currently Playing/Coming up widget to confirm what is being played out and what is next in the playlist. You can choose the right time to introduce an unordered break or to decide on the next action from the button box (TAKE NEXT, LOOP, UNLOOP, HOLD, UNHOLD). You can also detect issues in the channel output so that you can intervene, to view various parameters such as audio decibel level, SCTE information, video attributes, etc.

You can use the Missing Assets widget to monitor the asset in the playlist. This widget will help you to determine if the asset is missing from the player or is not uploaded to the cloud. If it is missing, you can download the asset from the cloud to the player.

The playlist widget lists the live segments and breaks separately along with the controls for action (enable/disable, play etc). This is always in the edit mode. You can edit the playlist even during the live playout and it can be published.

You can use the Shotbox widget to schedule unscheduled breaks [SCTE, graphics]. While doing so, you can use a marker to indicate which asset has to play next after the SCTE break.

How to set up a branded recording configuration.

How to set up a VOD profile for Live recording

What you need to be aware of

Branded recording parameter is enabled in the input BXF file by the channel distributor. Also, type of branded recording for a channel is specified.

  • Show Record (entire show along with graphics, captions etc is recorded)
  • One VOD file is generated with Live segments and breaks with 30 seconds of padding before live on and 30 seconds of padding after live off.
  • Conditioned (with soft segments)
  • One VOD file is generated with Live segments and 30 seconds of padding after live off.
  • Segmented Record (breaks are excluded)
  • One VOD file is generated for each Live segment. They can be used separately or in a combined mode. 30 seconds of padding is available after each live off.

Operator intervention is not required during the process of setting up the branded recording.

  • When recording is happening SCTE triggers are used to mark Live segment start and end.
  • Currently a delay of 9 seconds arises in the recorded event due to a processing delay and conversion to a SRT stream.
  • Hence every media asset under a break will have a delay of 9 seconds in the recorded SOM.
  • Recording done with a SHOW RECORD configuration is preserved as is.
  • Recording done with a SEGMENTED RECORD configuration can be edited.
  • As a Scheduler, you can manually edit the SOM and EOM for a break and schedule any other breaks.

  • UTC time displays the time at the Live Event for e.g.: Greenwich Time for English Premier League.
  • Operator time is the timezone in which the operator is.

How to view the clocks in the different time zones.

  1. Go to the Schedule, Playout or Live Playout page of the UI.
  2. At the top of the UI, you can see the different clocks in various time zones.
  3. The clocks show the Channel Timestamp, the Current time in the operator's region and the UTC or GMT.

Glossary of Terms

 

Actual Duration

Actual Duration is the time interval for which an asset or playlist item has been factually played out for. It is displayed in HH:MM:SS format, which stands for Hours:Minutes:Seconds. This is a non-editable display.

Actual Start Time

Actual Start Time is an attribute of an item of the playlist which is displayed  in the date format HH:MM:SS. It is a non-editable display. It indicates the actual start time of play of a playlist item.

Asset

An Asset is any audio/video/graphic stored in the media library. It could be a program series, promo, commercials, or live feed. This information is used for configuring automation for logo, graphics, identifying commercial breaks or scheduling DTMF/SCTE.

Asset Duration

Asset duration measures the duration of time for which the asset is produced for. It is specified in the HH:MM:SS:F format, which stands for Hours:Minutes:Seconds:Number of  Frames of the asset.

Auto Live On

Refers to a parameter setting for a channel, wherein the user can choose to go Live at a scheduled time in an automatic manner.

Break

Break refers to an item on the playlist, wherein a commercial, promo or graphic is played.

Channel

Channel is a service provided using a particular frequency.

Channel Broadcast Time

Channel broadcast time refers to the time at which broadcast begins for a channel. It is a parameter set during channel configuration.

 

Commercials

Commercials are videos which can be scheduled automatically or added manually in a playlist.

Commercial Minutes

Commercial Minutes is a widget available in the LIVE Playout Menu. It helps the user to know the aggregate minutes of commercials that have been scheduled for every hour.

CP Live

CLOUDPORT Live is a widget which provides a low resolution view of the input feed to the user.

Currently Playing

Currently Playing refers to the widget on the screen which shows a perspective of the currently playing asset from the playlist.

Dependent Graphic

A graphic asset which is played at an offset to another asset is a dependent graphic.

Disable Asset

Disable asset is part of the Button Bank controls on the screen. It is used to stop the asset from playing.

Dynamic Parameters

Refers to the set of parameters used in graphics settings.

Graphics

Graphics are assets such as logos, lower third banners, promos, dynamic graphics, tickers, countdowns or full screen cards.

Hold

Hold is the default attribute of a live segment by virtue of which the live segment continues to play even beyond the scheduled duration. It also forms part of the button bank controls on the user interface which help the user to intervene in the Live playout.

Independent graphic

Graphics for which the start time on the playlist, is not dependent on the asset prior to it.

Input Source 

Input source can be either a video file or Live feed.

Live Event

Live event is an event which is a currently happening occurrence of a show. For example:- sports matches, awards or a ceremony.

Live/ Non Live

Refer to the status of the system. System is in live state when the playout is happening. System is in a non live state when a live show or event is not being broadcast.

Live Playout

Refers to the menu in amagi DYNAMIC used for monitoring a playout.

Live Segment

Refers to the live feed as an item of the playlist, which is segmented (made into chunks) to play interspersed with breaks. Segmentation of files is done for faster playout and ease of handling.

Live Stream

Live stream refers to the Live coverage of an event over the internet.

Live Templates

Live Templates are guides to construct a Live event. Parameter settings in a template can be varied to design different Live events.

LOOP

Refers to the function of playing an asset in a cyclic manner until further action is taken by the user.

Manual Take

Manual Take is part of the button bank controls on the screen which help the user to intervene in the live playout. Manual Take is an attribute of a break item on the playlist.

Marker

Refers to the flag that can be set for an item on the playlist as a label. It enables the user to resume the playout at a designated item on the playlist.

Missing Assets

Assets that are not available on the cloud storage or downloaded on the player are missing assets.

Offset

Offset time refers to a delay in the start time of an item in the playlist. Offset time links an asset with another asset prior to it. It is specified in seconds.

Overrun

Overrun is the time difference between the duration of play of an asset and the scheduled duration for the asset. A break can have one or more assets played under it, in this scenario overrun refers to the difference between the duration of all the assets in the break and the scheduled duration of the break.

Overrun is displayed as a positive number.

Overrun is displayed at two stages

  • Scheduling
  1. Event Level

Scheduled duration of all playlist items - Scheduled duration of the event.

  1. Break Level

Scheduled duration of a break - Scheduled duration of all media assets grouped under the break.

  • Playout or LIVE Playout
  1. Event Level

Actual duration of play of the event - Scheduled duration of the event.

  1. Break Level

Actual duration of play of a break - Scheduled duration of all media assets grouped under the break.

  1. Live Segment Level

Actual duration of play of the live segment - Scheduled duration of Live segment.

Play Asset

Refers to the button bank control available to the user for playing a particular asset. It is present at each item level in a playlist.

 

Playlist

Playlist is a list of assets arranged to play in a linear manner. It consists of a combination of Live segments, Pre-recorded videos, commercials, promos and graphics.

Primary Asset

Refers to an asset in the hierarchy based on which certain parameters like grouping of commercials, promos or graphics is done. It also has relevance in linking a secondary asset like a graphic to a primary asset on the playlist.

Scheduled Duration

Scheduled Duration is the time interval for which a playlist item is scheduled to play for. It is specified in HH:MM:SS:F format, which stands for Hours:Minutes:Seconds:Number of Frames.

Scheduled Start Time

Scheduled Start Time is an attribute of an item of the playlist, which could be a Live segment or a break. It is editable and is in the format HH:MM:SS which stands for Hours:Minutes:Seconds. It indicates the time at which an asset is programmed to start.

SCTE

Refers to the industry broadcast standard for inserting cue tones in a transmitting stream. Full form is Society of Cable and Telecommunications Engineers.

Secondary Asset

Refers to the asset lower in order in a hierarchy of grouping of items in a playlist. This asset is linked or grouped to a primary asset.  

Secondary Graphic

Refers to a secondary asset which is a graphic item on the playlist.

Shotbox

Shotbox is a widget which aids in adding ad hoc breaks to a Live Event. Using this a user can play commercials, promos or graphics in an impromptu manner. Ad hoc SCTE breaks can also be played.  

Take Live

Refers to the menu by which a user can start a Live stream in an immediate manner.

TAKE NEXT

TAKE NEXT is a widget from the Button Bank Controls on the screen. It helps the user to alter the order of play in the playlist. On selection, the current item that is being played is stopped and the Next Item on the playlist is played out.

TGA

Refers to the industry standard raster graphic format used for digital media storage and distribution.

Timers

Timers constitute the display widgets on the screen, which present a countdown to milepost events of the playlist.

Triggers

Refer to the activation mechanism of a SCTE break. This menu tab is available under the media library widget.

Underrun

Underrun is the time difference between the duration of play of an asset and the scheduled duration for the asset. A break can have one or more assets played under it, in this scenario underrun refers to the difference between the duration of all the assets in the break and the scheduled duration of the break.

Underrun is displayed at two stages

  • Scheduling
  1. Event Level

Scheduled duration of all playlist items - Scheduled duration of the event.

  1. Break Level

Scheduled duration of a break - Scheduled duration of all media assets

grouped under the break.

  • Playout or LIVE Playout
  1. Event Level

Actual duration of play of the event - Scheduled duration of the event.

  1. Break Level

Actual duration of play of a break - Scheduled duration of all media assets grouped under the break.

  1. Live Segment Level

Actual duration of play of the live segment - Scheduled duration of Live segment.

  • In the schedule menu, if a media asset is not linked to a break, it displays as an underrun for the duration of the break.
  • In the schedule menu, if a media asset is scheduled for a shorter duration than the asset duration, then underrun is displayed in the number of frames.

Underrun is displayed as a negative number.

UNHOLD

Refers to the status of a Live segment item of the playlist. On checking the status to UNHOLD, the current Live segment stops playing and transfers control to the required item on the playlist.

UNLOOP

Refers to the button bank controls on the screen. It is available in the Edit Ribbon and also next to each break on the scheduled playlist.

Unordered Breaks 

Unordered breaks refer to the breaks which have been scheduled during LIVE Playout in an ad hoc manner. These breaks are scheduled using the ShotBox feature of Amagi CloudPort Live or Amagi Dynamic.

Video Asset

Refers to the media which is a pre-recorded content.
